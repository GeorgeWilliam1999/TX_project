# F4b — v8r1 bake-off: the chart LOSES to extrapUTT on the real field (2026-06-14)

## Result (deployment-relevant: real v8r1 field, PV-pointing pool, 4000 tracks)

| candidate | median dx | p95 dx | p99 dx | median dtx |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| straight line | 237,109 µm | 1.37 m | 1.77 m | 90,468 µrad |
| chart, O12 rebuilt on v8r1 | **4,725 µm** | 137 mm | 301 mm | 1,792 µrad |
| chart, best-tuned (O20 tight) | 3,868 µm | 114 mm | — | — |
| **extrapUTT (pol −1)** | **14.9 µm** | 2.2 mm | 11.7 mm | 5.7 µrad |

**extrapUTT beats the chart ~260–320×** on the real field. The chart's 12.1 µm headline
was an artifact of the **toy `twodip` field**; it does not transfer to v8r1.

By |q/p| quartile (low→high p), median dx µm: extrapUTT [749 / 46 / 4.6 / 1.1];
chart v8r1 [51212 / 7198 / 2445 / 791]. The chart is worse at **every** momentum,
including the highest (791 µm where there is almost no bend).

## Root cause: the real field is not flattenable by a GLOBAL even-multipole basis
Multipole reconstruction error of B_y over the chord region (x∈±2000, y∈±1500, z 2665→7826):

| field | ⟨\|B_y\|⟩ | ⟨\|residual\|⟩ | max\|residual\| | rel error |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| toy twodip | 0.678 T | 0.014 T | 0.14 T | **2.1 %** |
| real v8r1 | 0.776 T | 0.063 T | 1.73 T | **8.1 %** |

The O12 even-polynomial-per-z chart represents the toy field to ~2 % but v8r1 only to
~8 % → the analytic kick is ~8 % wrong → mm-scale endpoint error. Counter-intuitively the
v8r1 field is *flatter on-axis* (B_y(x=2000)/B_y(0)=0.85 vs toy 0.50), but it carries
**localised transverse structure** (fringe/edge fields; max residual 1.73 T) that a global
degree-≤20 polynomial cannot fit — classic Runge ringing at the window edges. **Raising the
order does not help** (O12→O20+tuning: 4725→3868 µm only).

extrapUTT wins because it is **not** a global expansion: it fits a **degree-9 polynomial per
60×50 (x,y) bin** (`compute_state`, biquadratic bin interpolation), so it adapts locally to
exactly this structure. That is the price of real-field accuracy.

## Why the toy result was misleading (and now corrected)
- The whole gen-1→3 corpus + the chart tables were built on the toy field, which is
  artificially smooth (essentially a clean single dipole). On a smooth field the global
  multipole flattening is near-exact (5.7 µm true-field floor, 12.1 µm tabulated chart).
- The real LHCb field is not that smooth. The flattening *principle* (analytic kick from a
  field representation) is sound; the *global-multipole representation* is the weak link.

## Implications
- **F5 deployment of the current chart is not justified** — it loses to the incumbent it was
  meant to replace, on the incumbent's own (real) field. The ADR 0011 "weights by another
  name" argument still holds in principle, but the chart as built is not competitive.
- **To compete, the chart needs a LOCALISED field representation** (splines / per-bin fits)
  rather than a single global polynomial per z-plane — which converges toward extrapUTT's
  own design. That is a chart redesign (new F-phase), not a re-tune.
- The toy-field 12.1 µm result remains valid as a *proof of principle on a smooth field*, but
  it is not the deployment number and should be labelled as such going forward.

## Feasibility probe — would a LOCALISED representation rescue it? (06-15)
Reconstruction error of v8r1 B_y over the chord region (x∈±2200, y∈±1600, z 2665→7826):

| representation | size | ⟨\|res\|⟩ (mean) | **median \|res\|** | max\|res\| |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| global O12 even-multipole (current) | 63.5 kB | 9.4 % | **3.6 %** | 2.16 T |
| coarse grid 13×9×40 + trilinear | 18 kB | 6.1 % | **0.88 %** | 2.13 T |
| coarse grid 17×13×60 + trilinear | 52 kB | 4.6 % | **0.37 %** | 1.90 T |
| binned 6 x-bins, deg(3,2)/z | 163 kB | 8.8 % | 3.2 % | 1.83 T |

A coarse 3-D grid + smooth interpolation cuts the **median** reconstruction error ~10× vs
the global multipole (0.37 % at 52 kB, still ~18× smaller than extrapUTT's ~960 kB tables).
So a localised chart would likely move the median from ~4.7 mm toward the ~0.1–1 mm range —
a large gain, but **parity with extrapUTT (14.9 µm) is NOT demonstrated**.

The hard wall is real, not representational: the v8r1 field has genuine sharp transverse
structure — verified against the raw `.bin` (FieldV8R1's trilinear matches grid nodes exactly),
on-axis \|B_y(0,y,5000)\| climbs −1.03 T (y=0) → −2.02 T (y=1800) non-monotonically, raw peak
**4.94 T at (x=−1600,y=1100,z=4300)** — and PV-pointing tracks DO traverse it (chord
\|y_end\| up to 1725 mm). Every smooth representation keeps a ~2 T max residual there; that
region is also extrapUTT's worst quartile (749 µm). The chart's distinctive pitch
(0 trained params, tiny tables, canonical-momentum structure) now reduces to "a cheaper
extrapUTT" — worth pursuing only if it matches extrapUTT accuracy at a real footprint win.

## Note on conventions resolved here
- `get_field_numpy(polarity=-1)` returns +B_y on-axis; `FieldV8R1` returns −B_y (the raw
  down-map). The two pipelines have OPPOSITE sign — the v8r1 chart's κ0 and C tables are both
  built from `FieldV8R1`, so they are internally consistent (κ0 ≈ +1.0e-3, the physical
  Lorentz constant for B in Tesla). The 1000× gap vs the toy κ0 (1.01e-6) is a units artifact
  of `get_field_numpy` (toy By is ~1000× the Tesla value in its internal scaling).
